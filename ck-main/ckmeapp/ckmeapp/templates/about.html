CK stands for Complete Knowledge. A PoCK (Proof of Complete Knowledge) may be viewed as a strengthened variant of a conventional proof of knowledge. It shows not just knowledge of a secret (witness), but fully unencumbered knowledge, i.e., unrestricted ability to use the secret for any desired purpose.

Why does it matter? Proofs of knowledge (PoKs) do not actually prove knowledge in cases where a prover may interact with other entities, which introduces a range of coercive attacks. Whereas integrating PoCK into existing protocols to safeguard users from coercion.


Possible attacks CKing your wallet can protect against:
Deniable Messaging: Require CK proof of forgeability with message
Voting: Require PoCK on vote key for ability to fake vote / re-vote with vote
Bribery: Require PoCK on funds key for ability to equivocate against briber

If you don’t use CK, your blockchain protocol is vulnerable to bribery/coercion. If you use CK, it helps in the design of protocols resistant to coercive attacks.
